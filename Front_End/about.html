Learning Objectives

  • Projectile Motion: Understand the relationship between initial velocity (v₀), launch angle (θ), initial height (h₀), and gravitational acceleration (g).
  • Circular Motion: Explore centripetal acceleration, banking angles, friction, and safe speed ranges for curved paths.
  • Energy Concepts: Learn about kinetic energy, potential energy, and energy conservation in mechanical systems.
  • Numerical Simulation: Experience browser-based physics simulations with real-time visualization and parameter control.
  • Self-Assessment: Reinforce concepts through interactive quizzes with instant feedback and performance tracking.

Key Physics Equations

Projectile Motion

Horizontal position: x(t) = v₀ cos θ · t

Vertical position: y(t) = h₀ + v₀ sin θ · t − ½g t²

Range: R = (v₀² sin 2θ) / g (when h₀ = 0)

Maximum height: h_max = h₀ + (v₀² sin² θ) / (2g)

Circular Motion

Centripetal acceleration: a_c = v² / r

Angular velocity: ω = v / r

Period: T = 2πr / v

Ideal bank angle: θ = arctan(v² / (rg))
