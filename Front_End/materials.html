Uniform Linear Motion (GLB)

Learning Video

Explanation

Uniform Linear Motion (GLB) is the motion of an object along a straight path with constant velocity. Imagine you're driving a car on a straight highway and the speedometer shows 80 km/h continuously without changing. That's GLB!

GLB Characteristics:

  • Constant velocity - Does not increase or decrease
  • Acceleration = 0 - No change in velocity
  • Distance proportional to time - The longer it moves, the farther the distance
  • Straight path - No turning

Main Formula:

s = distance traveled (meters)

v = velocity (m/s)

t = time (seconds)

Tips for Understanding GLB:

1. Remember the keyword "CONSTANT" - GLB velocity never changes
2. The v-t graph is a horizontal line - Because the velocity is constant
3. The s-t graph is a straight diagonal line - Shows linear relationship

Projectile Motion (Parabolic)

Learning Video

Explanation

Projectile Motion is the motion of an object thrown at an angle through the air, following a curved (parabolic) path. Examples include a basketball shot, a kicked football, or water from a fountain.

Projectile Motion Characteristics:

  • Parabolic trajectory - Creates a curved path
  • Two-dimensional motion - Has horizontal (x) and vertical (y) components
  • Constant horizontal velocity - No acceleration in x-direction
  • Vertical acceleration = g - Gravity affects the vertical component

Uniform Circular Motion

Learning Video

Explanation

Uniform Circular Motion is the motion of an object traveling in a circular path at constant speed. Examples include a car turning on a banked curve, satellites orbiting Earth, or a ball on a string being swung in a circle.

Circular Motion Characteristics:

  • Constant speed - Magnitude of velocity doesn't change
  • Changing direction - Velocity direction constantly changes
  • Centripetal acceleration - Always points toward the center
  • Centripetal force required - Keeps object in circular path
